That these gallants were an unmitigated nuisance, and had frequently to be silenced by the common people who came to enjoy the play, seems certain. Dekker's Gull's Hornbook (1609) has an interesting chapter on "How a Gallant should behave Himself in a Playhouse."

The first actors were classed with thieves and vagabonds; but they speedily raised their profession to an art and won a reputation which extended far abroad.
