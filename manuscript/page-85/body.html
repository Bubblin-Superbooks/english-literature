Springs of Anglo-Saxon Poetry In reading Anglo-Saxon poetry it is well to remember these five principles, for they are like the little springs at the head of a great river,—clear, pure springs of poetry, and out of them the best of our literature has always flowed. Thus when we read,

Blast of the tempest—it aids our oars;
Rolling of thunder—it hurts us not;
Rush of the hurricane—bending its neck
To speed us whither our wills are bent,
