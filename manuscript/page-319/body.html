So the fear of hell was ingrained into an ignorant people for four centuries. Alternating with these horrors were bits of rough horse-play and domestic scenes of peace and kindliness, representing the life of the English fields and homes. With these were songs and carols, like that of the Nativity, for instance:

As I out rode this enderes (last night, )

Of three jolly shepherds I saw a sight,
And all about their fold a star shone bright;
They sang terli terlow, So merryly the shepherds their pipes can blow.
